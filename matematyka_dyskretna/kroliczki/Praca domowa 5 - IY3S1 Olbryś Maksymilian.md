## 10/122

Dla $f: \mathbb{R}^2 \ni \left< x, y \right> \mapsto \left< 2 \cdot x, 3 \cdot y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2$ wyznacz obrazy zbiorów $\mathbb{N}^2$ oraz $\left\{ \left< x,y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2 : x^2 + y^2 \leq 1 \right\}$

**a)**
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\vec{f} &= \left\{\left< x, y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2: \exists \left< n, m \right>  \in \mathbb{N}^2: f(\left< n, m \right> ) = \left< 2 \cdot n, 3 \cdot m \right> = \left< x, y \right>   \right\} \\
&= \left\{ \left< x, y \right> \in \mathbb{N}^2: 2 | x \wedge 3 | y  \right\} 
\end{array}
$$

**b)**
Niech $X = \left\{ \left< x, y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2 : x^2 + y^2 \leq 1 \right\}$
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\vec{f} &= \left\{ \left< x, y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2: \exists \left< a, b \right> \in X: f(\left< a, b \right>) = \left< 2a, 3b \right> = \left< x, y \right>    \right\}
\end{array}
$$
Widać więc że 
$$
\begin{array}{l}
a = \frac{x}{2} \\
b = \frac{y}{3}
\end{array}
$$
zatem
$$
\left< \frac{x}{2}, \frac{y}{3} \right> \in X \implies \frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{y^2}{9} \leq 1
$$
Co z kolei daje nam:
$$
\vec{f} = \left\{ \left< x, y \right> \in \mathbb{R}^2: \frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{y^2}{9} \leq 1 \right\} 
$$
